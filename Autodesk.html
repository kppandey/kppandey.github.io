Autodesk

Improving Design Platforms

As a UI Developer, I worked with the HCI & Vis research team to develop an advanced React app prototype for Autodesk 3D modeling softwares.

About HCI & VIS Research Team

A team of expert scientists with a goal to explore and create technologies to improve design through high-level projects and collaboration with leading research universities. Curious about Autodesk Research? Find more projects here!

Problem

At Autodesk, we noticed two main problems of our design platforms:

  1. Loss of knowledge that occurs when iterating designs.
  2. There is no seamless communication tool for collaboration.

Design Process

The main problem I was trying to solve was: How can we incorporate communication and capture knowledge in design platforms to improve the entire design experience?

I first conducted research to better understand Autodesk users. I read through existing documents in Autodesk's help desk, Slack channels and interviewed developers of the softwares.

Pain Points

  1. Users cannot track their design and see their previous edits when iterating.
  2. Collaboration is inconvenient.
  3. Users cannot communicate with collaborators during design progress.

Solution

Due to the short timeframe I had, I decided to focus on one solution: to allow users to communicate with other collaborators and track their progress as they design.

Design Iterations

  1. Review competitors features and other similar UI patterns.
  2. Study existing designs in the Autodesk system to have more context on prior visual style decisions.
  3. Draw low-fidelity mockups for a general outline on how to organize the information and tools.
  4. Create high-fidelity solutions and collaborate with other computer scientists and engineers for internal iterations.

Challenges

Determining how to incorporate communication in these softwares.
Since the goal of this prototype was to allow users to collaborate with their colleagues and track their design iterations, I decided that the best solution would be to use Slack API. I created an add-in application that will allow users to connect their designs to Slack within Autodesk softwares.

Deciding on the simplest experience for the user to connect to Slack through the Autodesk software.
After conducting A/B testing, I found that the easist solution was to immediately connect to Slack after the user creates a new design.

Technical Challenges.
For this project, I had to not only design the prototype but I had to develop it as well. It was quite a challenge but learning React was one of my key highlights for this internship. Additionally, I was able to gain a lot of knowledge in backend development. I had to understand how to deploy a React app on AWS, Docker and Jenkins.
